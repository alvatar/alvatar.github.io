Álvaro Castro-Castilla

Hello. I am Álvaro Castro-Castilla (@alvatar). An architect, software engineer and artist.

I work at the intersection of traditional techniques like acrylic and oil with cutting-edge software technology. The result of this are Digital Entities made of Self-Modifying Code, Neural Networks, Cellular Automata and Evolutionary Computations.

From 2001 to 2010, digital art & architecture were my primary interests, and so I created my first digital art projects while still studying at Escuela Politécnica de Arquitectura de Madrid. This was a time that didn't know about social networks, the iPhone, or many of the creative tools available nowadays. I coded in C/C++, CUDA and OpenGL, because I always believed that you needed to be close to the metal to create purely digital beings with a real understanding of their internals. That was required to feel and understand what makes software entities exist in their own electrical universe. Some of the things I worked on have now become commonplace, like neural networks or touch devices. However, during nearly a decade (2010-2019), I stayed away of creating art, while focused almost exclusively on software engineering and entrepreneurial projects. But in 2019, I felt again the need to continue the journey to shape a language in art combining the engineering and technical knowledge I've acquired over the years, together with my passion for fine arts.
